1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Account information: email address, date of birth (used to verify age — see Section 13), display name, optional gender
  • Profile data: height, weight, dietary goals, activity level, dietary restrictions or allergies (if you choose to share)
  • Food data: photos of meals, text descriptions, audio recordings of meals
  • Health-related data: calorie intake, macro and micro nutrients, dietary patterns, body measurements over time

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device information: device model, operating system version, language, timezone, unique device identifiers
  • Usage data: App interactions, features used, session duration, crash logs, performance metrics
  • Attribution & analytics data: install source, aggregated usage events (sessions, feature usage, retention cohorts) via Adjust GmbH (Berlin, Germany)

1.3 Information from Third Parties

  • App Store / Google Play: purchase and subscription status
  • Sign-in providers: if you use Apple Sign-In or Google Sign-In, we receive name and email as authorized by you
  • Health platforms (optional): if you connect Apple HealthKit or Google Health Connect, we may receive activity, weight, or other data you choose to share

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Provide the Service: analyze food photos, text and audio using AI to estimate nutritional content
  • Personalize experience: tailor calorie goals and recommendations based on your profile
  • Process payments: manage subscriptions through app store billing
  • Improve AI accuracy: with your opt-in, use pseudonymised food photo data to improve our recognition models (see Section 3.3 — you can opt out at any time)
  • Send notifications: reminders, streak updates, and service-related communications (marketing only with your opt-in consent)
  • Provide support: respond to your requests and troubleshoot issues
  • Analytics: understand how users interact with the App to improve it
  • Security & fraud prevention: detect and prevent unauthorized access, abuse, and policy violations
  • Legal compliance: comply with applicable laws and regulations

3. AI Processing and Automated Decision-Making

3.1 How AI Analyzes Your Data

When you submit a food photo, text, or audio:

  • The data is sent to our servers (or third-party AI providers) for analysis
  • AI models estimate the nutritional content of the food
  • Results are returned to the App and stored in your account
  • Typical accuracy is approximately ±15-25% relative to laboratory analysis. Accuracy depends on photo quality, food complexity, portion-size estimation, and visibility of ingredients.

3.2 AI Service Providers

We use the following third-party AI services to process food recognition:

  • OpenAI, L.L.C. — image analysis, hosted in USA (EU-US Data Privacy Framework certified; SCCs signed)
  • Anthropic, PBC — text/voice analysis, hosted in USA (EU-US Data Privacy Framework certified; SCCs signed)

These providers process your data solely for the purpose of providing nutritional analysis under signed Data Processing Agreements (Article 28 GDPR). An up-to-date list of sub-processors is available at: yumisa.app/sub-processors

3.3 AI Model Improvement — Opt-Out

By default, we may use pseudonymised food photos (with personal identifiers removed) to improve our AI recognition models. You can opt out at any time in Settings → Privacy → AI Improvement. Opting out does not affect your access to the Service.

3.4 Automated Decision-Making (GDPR Art. 22 / CCPA ADMT)

Yumisa uses automated processing to estimate nutritional content. This is an informational tool only — it does not produce legal effects or similarly significant decisions about you. You have the right to:

  • Request a human review of any specific result
  • Object to AI-based processing
  • Receive a plain-language explanation of how the AI works

3.5 EU AI Act

Yumisa is an AI system within the meaning of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 ("EU AI Act"). We classify food recognition as a limited-risk AI system. We comply with applicable transparency obligations, including disclosing that you are interacting with an AI system and informing you about its capabilities and limitations.

6. Data Security and Health Breach Notification

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data, including:

  • Encryption in transit: TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Encryption at rest: AES-256
  • Authentication: secure password hashing (bcrypt/Argon2), OAuth 2.0 for sign-in providers
  • Access controls: role-based access, principle of least privilege, audit logging
  • Network security: firewalls, intrusion detection, DDoS protection
  • Operational security: regular security assessments, vulnerability scanning, employee security training

In the event of a personal data breach affecting your information, we will notify you and, where required, regulatory authorities in accordance with applicable law, including:

  • GDPR Art. 33-34: notification to supervisory authority within 72 hours; notification to affected users without undue delay if breach poses high risk
  • FTC Health Breach Notification Rule (HBNR): notification within 60 days for US users
  • State breach notification laws: as applicable

No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.
